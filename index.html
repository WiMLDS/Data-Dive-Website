Mission Statement

What do we want? High quality, affordable healthcare. What do we have? Well, not that. So, let’s fix it. Data can shine a light on what’s wrong with US healthcare. We’ll get together to look at public health-related data to diagnose the US healthcare system: we know the symptoms, can we find the cause?

  • Learn: Learn about the US healthcare system and open source data analysis tools that you can use to work with healthcare data.
  • Improve: Explore the drivers of healthcare cost and brainstorm with us towards a better future for US healthcare.
  • Collaborate: Work with others to achieve a common goal. Learn from the person next to you. Healthcare concerns all of us, let’s fix it together.

Data Sets

CMS Open Payments

Detailed transactions between a physician or hospital and a pharma company or device maker. 2013 and 2014 data is available, summing up to $3.5 billion.

Learn More

Elhadad dataset

Datasets consist of information on articles from biomedical and health journals published by Reuters for the purpose of predicting what health care news gets picked up by the media.

Learn More

2010 Chronic Conditions PUFs

This dataset contains aggregated information from 2010 Medicare claims. Each record is a profile - consisting of age, gender, chronic conditions indicators, dual eligibility status indicators. For each profile, claims related variables are provided as averages.

Learn More

Malpractice claims

A sample of malpractice claims from the National Healthcare Practitioner Data Bank containing information about payment amounts and the specific malpractice reasons, complete with geolocation data and practitioner license type. Data aggregated through enigma.io.

Learn More

Medicare Provider Utilization and Payment Data

Contains information on services and procedures provided to Medicare beneficiaries by physicians and other healthcare professionals, including information about the physician, the procedure, payment allowed amount and Medicare payment, complete with geolocation data.

Learn More

SPARCS

Contains NY state hospital inpatient information. 2009 through 2013 hospital discharges data is available at the (de-identified) patient level and facility level. Length of stay, diagnostic, procedure information available.
